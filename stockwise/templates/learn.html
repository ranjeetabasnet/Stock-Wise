📚 Free Learning Resources

The best free places to keep learning about investing — hand-picked for beginners.

📖 Learn to invest
🌐
Investopedia — Investing Essentials FREE
The go-to reference for every investing term. Clear definitions, examples, and beginner guides on everything from stocks to ETFs.
 🎓
Khan Academy — Personal Finance FREE
Structured video courses covering stocks, bonds, mutual funds, and how markets work. Great for absolute beginners.
 🏛️
SEC Investor Education FREE
Official guides from the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission on how to invest safely and avoid fraud.
📊 Market data & research
📈
Yahoo Finance FREE
Live stock prices, financials, analyst ratings, and news. One of the most comprehensive free data sources available.
 🗺️
Finviz — Stock Screener FREE
Visual stock screener and market heatmap. Great for seeing which sectors are up or down at a glance.
 📉
Macrotrends — Long-term Charts FREE
Historical charts going back decades for any stock or index. Perfect for understanding long-term trends.
🎙️ Podcasts & video
▶️
Graham Stephan — YouTube FREE
Plain-English explanations of investing, real estate, and personal finance. Popular with young first-time investors.
 🎙️
NPR Planet Money Podcast FREE
Short, story-driven episodes that make economics and markets genuinely entertaining. Great for commutes.
 ▶️
Investopedia — YouTube FREE
Short explainer videos on every investing concept. Perfect companion to their written guides.
📕 Books (widely available)
📗
The Little Book of Common Sense Investing — Bogle
The bible of index fund investing. Short, clear, and life-changing for beginners. Often available at local libraries.
 📘
A Random Walk Down Wall Street — Malkiel
Classic guide explaining why markets are hard to beat and why diversified index funds are the smart choice for most people.
